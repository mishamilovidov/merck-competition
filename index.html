Recommendation

Developing countries have a drastically higher undiagnosed and death rates compared to developed countries. We recommend that Merck Medical develop an inexpensive and accurate pre-screening solution for individuals who feel they might have diabetes. A pre-screening solution would be particularly effective in developing countries where finding a doctor or hospital to diagnose diabetes is either financially or logistically impractical.

Conclusion

Developing countries lack the medical infrastructure necessary to properly diagnose, treat, and prevent diabetes. Inadequate medical infrastructure has caused an alarmingly high rate of undiagnosed diabetes in developing countries, which currently account for 80% of diabetes cases globally. By developing an inexpensive product that can screen for diabetes, we can begin to properly educate and treat diabetic individuals and lower diabetic related deaths across the globe.
